My definition of chic:

Elevated, true to the self, embracing of imperfection. Lived-in and realistic, put together, interesting. A Wabi Sabi curation of selfhood. It's not just how you look, it's how you carry yourself. 

##### Fashion

###### Rewearing the same clothes over and over

Without the need to impress or entertain other people with outfit choices. Consistency is very chic to me. It shows a lack of regard for both the internal and external voyeur, a true embracing of selfhood and emotional expression in a controlled and curated way. Rewearing clothes and not falling victim to trend cycles or fashion fads shows an understanding of personal taste, especially when the capsule outfits are untrendy. 

###### Silver tone jewellery 

Gold is more abundant, and has connotations of luxury associated with it. I find silver to be a sort of ugly stepsister here - less costly, less frequently seen on the rack, more understated. I find the understated to be *very chic*. This extends, possibly even more so, to steel or chrome. Cooler tones have a je ne sais quois to them for me personally. I also don't particularly care for colour theory when it comes to metals - there's something very self-assured about choosing to wear what is in line with your personal preferences rather than what makes your skin glow.

###### Vintage / Indie handbags

Clearly second hand or small business finds show off so much more about who you are than any first hand luxury bag ever could. I refuse to believe that anyone is buying a monogrammed never-full for any reason other than its connotations of wealth. The idea of luxury, to me, doesn't come from the concept of financial wealth so much as it does from the concept of great craftsmanship. I have two handbags; my Gucci Bamboo, and my work bag which is a large tote from Idea Books. Neither are particularly overstated, but I feel reflected by them both. I also find that having a smaller selection of bags that are really aligned with my taste is very chic of me. 

###### Cohesion

Having a visual rhythm is *very* chic to me. When everything just sort of works together, even in an inexplicable way. For me, I think it's wearing all black everything. I will occasionally make an exception for a little pop of white, grey, or an earth tone. I like to dress neutrally, and I find neutral colours to be optimum for achieving chicness.
