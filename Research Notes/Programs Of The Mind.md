As a child, we are programmed in both mind and body. We are taught love, ambition, pain - and, as pattern seeking animals, use these as filters for how we see the world. We are taught lessons, create boundaries, limitations, hopes and dreams. If we view the brain as a computer, we have these programs downloaded into our software. Our lived experiences download more programs into our subconscious mind, which impacts our body. For example, a child that is raised in scarcity will most likely live as an adult in scarcity - living paycheque to paycheque. Life experiences give us the information necessary to form beliefs and assumptions, but the habits and belief patterns we hold are not necessarily our own, thanks to epigenetics (the study of how external environments can alter genetic language and form tendencies, for example children of drug addicts are more likely to become addicts themselves through their environment making them more prone to addictive tendencies.)

An experiment was conducted in 2013 at Emory University, in which a male rat was administered electric shocks when cherry blossoms were present in the environment. The male rat was then reintroduced to a wider group of rats, impregnated a female rat, and the offspring produced, when they were able to smell cherry blossom, exhibited a fear response. Trained male mice, shocked when exposed to the scent, produced offspring that were fearful of the odour, despite having no direct experience of the trauma. The traumatic experience (scent + shock) induced physical changes in the sperm DNA, specifically altering the methylation of the Olfr151 (M71) gene responsible for sensing the scent.

Decisions we make today are filtered through the inherited genetic traits that are programmed into our subconscious. If you don't address the programs, they will turn into behaviours, which turn into personality traits. It's like walking through life with a box over your head. 

There is a three step process to undoing this programming:

1. Thinking
2. Doing
3. Being

Knowing what you need to do in your head, practicing it with your hands, and knowing it in your heart.

The language of the mind and the body are emotions. If your ultimate desire is to feel successful no matter what you are trying to do, if you want to live in abundance, you have to release limiting beliefs, and emotionally embrace what you can logically think. 

Science tells us we have 60-70,000 thoughts per day, and 90% of those are the same thoughts we had yesterday. This causes stagnation. The same thoughts lead to the same choices, which lead to the same behaviours, which lead to the same experiences, and the same experiences will inform the initial thoughts and the emotions attached to them. How you think, how you act, and how you feel form your personality. Decide if your thoughts belong in your future - you have to become greater than your environment.

1. Become conscious and aware of the program, and separate yourself from it and become an observer to the program. 
2. Modify behaviours and actions to be better. You have to consciously make different choices that align with a new thought process
3. Repeat

Your mind will slip back into old thoughts, because it is comfortable. Stepping into the unknown and making new choices is hard, because it is unpredictable. Suffering is predictable - we know the outcome. Your brain doesn't know the difference between the past, the present, and the future, meaning that we relive experiences when we think about them for too long. As the body adapts to new conditions created by new choices, this begins to feel normal. This is the hermetic treadmill.
