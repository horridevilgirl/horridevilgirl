**Shadow work is inherently archetypal.** 

The oldest myth on shadow work we have on record is the epic of Gilgamesh. In order to engage with shadow work, you have to love and embrace the horrid disgusting parts of yourself without feeling shame. You have to acknowledge and own the parts of you that are sometimes a bad person. Ignoring that is spiritual bypassing. Stop resisting it!!

Carl Jung: **until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate**

Being aware of your unconscious, shameful self takes away its power. Avoidance signals your belief that it's a bad thing, that it's shameful. When you can talk about and explore these parts of yourself and embrace them, you de-centre them from your life and bring power back to yourself and your ego in a truthful and meaningful. 

You were made in a creator's image, therefore you are a creator too. Everything has a mirror or a shadow - there is no light without darkness, no happiness without sadness. 

God is not necessarily loving or good, not everything is love and light. When we recognise the world is dualistic, and you can love both, that's when you can actually start to make peace with your own life. Because everything is dualistic and inherently perfect, that means that moralising good and bad is technically not real. Morals are created by people for people to control people. We need this so we're not all insane. Duality is real, and it is part of a divine order. We have both heaven and hell on this earth, and you get to choose whether to ascend and experience the heaven around you or choose to live in hell. Existence can be beautiful without needing any external changes to your life. 

Everything we see in the universe is also inside of us. We have access to both all of the goodness and all of the evil around us, within us as potential energy. All of us have the seed within us to be horrific and terrible, just as much as we have the potential to be beautiful and loving. 

In the **Bhagavad Gita** (old Hindu story) there is a conversation between a warrior and Krishna. The warrior gets to see what god looks like, and it's not love and light - it was so grotesque and so beautiful at once. Angels in the bible have to tell people to 'be not afraid' which would suggest that they are terrifying and terrific. The first step to shadow work is realising that you are this neutral vessel with the capability for good and bad. Any opposite has no inherent moralisation, that is a judgement or categorisation that we put on people. There is only right for you and wrong for you.

In terms of self discovery, we need to learn which archetypes of ourself we have learned to shame and repress. We learn which to repress based on what our parents think to be shameful, and in society as a whole. Cultures, countries, digital spaces, local community. We learn morals from all of these things. When we repress these and moralise a present part of ourself, it gains a lot of power over us. Think of it like trying to push an inflatable ball in a pool -it floats on the surface easily, but the further you push it down, the stronger the upward force becomes and the harder it is to keep it down. When you let the beach ball up, the further you've pressed down, the more dramatic its surfacing is. 

When you have a longstanding habit of people pleasing, saying 'no' is your beachball (taking up space, having boundaries, living in your own ego). Eventually, in relationships it becomes impossible to hold it down, and it surfaces as resentment. In trying to not be such a bitch, you kind of become a mega-bitch with the people who you feel confident and comfortable enough with to show parts of your shadow-self. The more effort you make to try and repress these elements of yourself, you set them into motion and create an inevitability.

**You're avoiding these things and yet they're still there!!!**

The more that you repress these things, the more noise they make when they come to the surface. 

You can find shadows through shame and judgement. Self-reflect on these aspects of yourself. Conduct the name-calling test. Imagine someone writes a call-out article on you, whatever you fear being called the most is your biggest or most prominent shadow. Look at the things you're deeply ashamed of - actions, ways of being etc etc. 

**How to do shadow work**

Own and revel in the parts of yourself that you are so ashamed of. Move energy from repression to love. 


**Common Shadow Archetypes (Unconscious/Unhealed Aspects)**

These archetypes represent the "dark" or disowned sides of personality: 

- **The Saboteur:** The inner voice that creates self-doubt, fear of failure, and undermines personal progress to maintain a false sense of safety.
- **The Victim:** Driven by feelings of helplessness or past trauma, this archetype avoids responsibility and seeks validation through sympathy or blaming others.
- **The Child:** Represents wounded, immature, or abandoned aspects, leading to defensive behaviour, lack of authority, or emotional volatility.
- **The Prostitute/Addict:** Manifests as "selling out" one's soul, values, or integrity for security, material gain, or addiction.

In owning that you will never be perfect, you accept yourself perfectly as you are. The acting out of these archetypes theatrically follow only two rules:
- it must be safe for you
- it must be safe for people around you

It doesn't matter what you do, only that you allow that part of you to exist (which it already does). Let these parts out in a way that is safe for yourself or they will control you. The shadow brings up scarcity mindset - nobody would ever love this part of me. **That's not true!!!!** There are a million and one ways to act out your perversions and hedonistic tendencies and still be loved and valued!

What you resist persists. If you allow yourself to not only own but enjoy elements of your shadow, it will weirdly start to dissipate. If the universe loved all the horrible things that happened, they literally would not exist. 

Going too far too fast can trigger this more though - acting out these practices and feeling immense shame can cause further repression. Focus on sensations.

1. notice the trigger, eg judging ugliness
2. name the archetype, eg fearing ugliness 
3. out-crazy the shadow, get uglier 
4. act it out, revel in ugliness 

Disassociation is a sign that you're feeling shameful and dis-regulated. Breathe through it if you can. This is the end of shadow work. Experience lightness and worthiness.
