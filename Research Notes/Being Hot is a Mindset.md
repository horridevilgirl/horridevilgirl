Beauty standards are real, but differ culture to culture, time period to time period. That means that they're not set in stone or inherent. This means that we have the power to influence them.

You can tell when someone is insecure from body language, from the way they walk and talk and interact with others. In **I Feel Pretty**, Amy Schuemer's character sees herself as a beautiful woman despite nothing changing in her life. When she thinks she's hot, she embodies that version of herself. By feeling different and believing she's hot, she attracts everyone around her and becomes more socially capable; her self perception changed, and that changed the entire world around her. 

When you feel different or less deserving, you begin to act like you are those things. When you open your mind to a different self perception, your #identity shifts, and then your reality shifts with it. The universe reflects back to you whatever you believe. When you start to see flaws as features, it reframes your whole life.

You do not have to fit societal expectations of what is hot. For me, what if my acne is not a flaw but a feature of my face. My short hair is not something to be self-conscious of, but something that is just a part of how I look. The rules are literally being made up as we go along. Where do we draw the line between 'unconventional beauty' and a feature of who we are? You can either label something as wrong or decide it's simply just there. This relates to [[Shadow Work]] in the sense that we're applying morality to parts of ourself. Every feature is just as good as it is bad, how we perceive it is up to us. Revel in the flaws, even if you believe them to be just that. Flaws are not inherently bad, and there is nothing wrong with being flawed. Neutralising your opinion of what it means to have flaws and to have 'ugly' features takes power away from them, de-centring them from your life and allowing you to embrace the transformative and fulfilling aspects of the ego. 

Mirror neurones are cells in our brain that fire when we do something, or when we see someone else do something. I.e when we see someone yawn, we yawn, when we see someone trip, we cringe for them. When you embody confidence and magnetism, it is mirrored back to you. The way that you see yourself is the way that other people see you. **We are pattern recognising animals and we can see our own behaviours in others. I know how I act when nervous or self-conscious and I can recognise those behaviours in other people, drawing attention to them in a 'negative' way**. 

How you feel > how you show up > how people see you. 

Reticular Activating System is a bundle of nerves that acts as a filter to the world. It decides what to filter in and out. When you're tuned in to something, you see it more. If you believe you're ugly, the RAS filters for confirmation of this. If you believe you're hot, your brain starts to string together every moment that reinforces this version of your self image. 

Hotness is great, ugliness is a virtue, nothing means anything until you decide it does.
