I kept meaning to go to that gallery on the canal. I kept meaning to learn the bus routes. I kept a list on my phone of restaurants I was saving for a special occasion that never came.

A furnished apartment is a kind of amnesia. Someone else's taste, someone else's dishes. You eat off their plates and sleep under their duvet and conduct your entire inner life in a space that does not know your name.

I have started to prefer it. The way you can leave without consequence. The way nothing accumulates.

Essay No. 1
On Beauty
A study in what we choose to call beautiful, and why we need so badly to call it something.
"Beauty is not in the thing — it is in the permission we give ourselves to stop."

There is a particular kind of beauty that only exists in passing. You catch it from a train window, or across a room, and the moment you turn to name it, it is already gone.

The theorists say beauty is a category. That we impose it on the world like a grid. But I think it works the other way — that beauty is what happens when the world briefly refuses the grid.

Essay No. 2
The Internet as Archive
Everything is still there. That is the problem.
"The web does not forget. We have confused this for memory."

There are photographs of me online from when I was sixteen that I cannot delete.

This is the texture of contemporary selfhood. Not a continuous narrative but a series of cached versions.
